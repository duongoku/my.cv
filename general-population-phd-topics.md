## ✅ Topic A: AI-Powered Personal Memory Assistant for Daily Life
**Inspired by:** MemPal, Memoro (lab's existing work)

**Research Question:** *How can multimodal AI help healthy adults remember and retrieve information in their daily lives?*

**What it would do:**
- Passive capture: Conversations, documents, photos, locations
- LLM-powered retrieval: "What did I discuss with my supervisor last week?"
- Contextual reminders: Proactive suggestions based on context
- Privacy-first design: User controls what's stored/how

**User studies (all feasible in 3 years):**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Diary study of memory needs in daily life
- **Study 2** (6 months): Prototype evaluation with 20 participants
- **Study 3** (8 months): Field deployment with 40 participants

**Why it's great:**
- Applies to EVERYONE (students, professionals, caregivers)
- Builds directly on lab's MemPal/Memoro work
- Hot topic: AI memory assistants are exploding (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
- Clear consumer product potential

**Publications:** CHI, IMWUT, CSCW, AAAI (AI for HCI)

## ✅ Topic B: Adaptive Attention Management for Productivity & Wellbeing
**Inspired by:** WearVibe, Prompto (lab's work on attention/cognitive load)

**Research Question:** *How can wearables + AI help people manage attention, reduce context-switching, and prevent burnout?*

**What it would do:**
- Monitor attention state: Focus, distracted, tired (via biosignals + behavior)
- Smart notifications: Suppress interruptions during deep focus
- Adaptive breaks: Suggest breaks at optimal times (like Prompto)
- Haptic guidance: Gentle nudges to return to task (like WearVibe)
- Productivity analytics: Help users understand their patterns

**User studies:**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Understanding attention challenges in knowledge workers
- **Study 2** (6 months): Prototype with 20 office workers/students
- **Study 3** (8 months): 2-week field study measuring productivity/wellbeing

**Why it's great:**
- MASSIVE problem: Everyone struggles with attention in digital age
- Builds on lab's WearVibe/Prompto expertise
- Relevant to students, remote workers, professionals
- Hot area: Digital wellbeing is huge (Apple Screen Time, etc.)

**Publications:** CHI, IMWUT, CSCW, digital wellbeing workshops

## ✅ Topic C: Personalized Sleep Enhancement for General Population
**Inspired by:** Sleep Enhancement project (lab's work)

**Research Question:** *How can we use wearables and sensory stimulation to improve sleep quality and memory for healthy adults?*

**What it would do:**
- Smartwatch sleep monitoring: Detect sleep stages (REM, deep sleep)
- Personalized interventions:
  - Auditory cues during optimal sleep phases for memory consolidation
  - Smart alarm: Wake during light sleep
  - Bedtime routine suggestions based on patterns
- Integration with daily schedules: Optimize sleep around work/school

**User studies:**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Sleep habits + intervention preferences
- **Study 2** (6 months): Pilot with 20 participants, 4 weeks each
- **Study 3** (8 months): RCT comparing intervention vs. control

**Why it's great:**
- Everyone wants better sleep
- Builds on lab's proven Sleep Enhancement work
- Short-term measurable effects (weeks, not years)
- Consumer product potential (Whoop, Oura, etc.)

**Publications:** IMWUT, CHI, sleep journals, digital health venues

## ✅ Topic D: Stress & Emotional Wellbeing Monitoring + Intervention
**Inspired by:** Total VREcall (biosignals for emotion), chatbot wellbeing research

**Research Question:** *How can multimodal biosensing detect stress states and deliver personalized interventions for emotional wellbeing?*

**What it would do:**
- Monitor stress signals: Heart rate variability, sleep, typing patterns, voice
- Contextual understanding: What situations trigger stress?
- Micro-interventions:
  - Breathing exercises via haptic guidance
  - Suggest breaks/activities based on stress patterns
  - Conversational AI for emotional support
- Long-term insights: Help users understand their stress triggers

**User studies:**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Stress patterns in daily life
- **Study 2** (6 months): Prototype interventions with 25 participants
- **Study 3** (8 months): Longer deployment measuring wellbeing outcomes

**Why it's great:**
- Universal problem: Everyone experiences stress
- Mental health is massively important
- Combines biosensing (fSense), chatbots (wellbeing research), interventions
- Real-world impact: Improve daily quality of life

**Publications:** CHI, IMWUT, CSCW, mental health/tech venues

## ✅ Topic E: AI-Powered Learning & Skill Acquisition Assistant
**New direction** - combines LLMs with learning science

**Research Question:** *How can AI optimize learning and skill acquisition for students and professionals?*

**What it would do:**
- Personalized learning paths: Adapt to individual learning styles/pace
- Spaced repetition optimization: Schedule reviews based on cognitive science
- Micro-learning moments: Detect when user has cognitive capacity for learning
- Explainable tutoring: LLMs that explain concepts in user's preferred style
- Multimodal learning: Integrate text, video, interactive exercises

**User studies:**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Learning challenges + preferences
- **Study 2** (6 months): Prototype with students learning new skills
- **Study 3** (8 months): Compare learning outcomes with/without AI assistant

**Why it's great:**
- Education is universal
- LLMs + learning science is cutting edge
- Applies to students, professionals learning new skills
- Huge market: EdTech is booming

**Publications:** CHI, learning tech conferences (L@S, AIED), educational AI

## ✅ Topic F: Digital Habit Formation & Behavior Change System
**Combines wellness, behavior science, AI**

**Research Question:** *How can wearables + AI help people form healthy habits and break unhealthy ones?*

**What it would do:**
- Habit tracking: Multimodal (behavior, context, biosignals)
- Personalized strategies: What works for THIS individual?
- Just-in-time interventions: Nudges at optimal moments
- Social elements: Community support, friendly competition
- Long-term behavior modeling: Understand habit formation patterns

**User studies:**
- **Study 1** (4 months): Habit challenges + current strategies
- **Study 2** (6 months): Prototype with people trying to form/break habits
- **Study 3** (8 months): Longitudinal study measuring habit formation

**Why it's great:**
- Universal: Everyone wants better habits
- Massive consumer interest (fitness apps, meditation apps, etc.)
- Combines behavior science with AI
- Real-world impact: Help people live healthier

**Publications:** CHI, IMWUT, CSCW, persuasive tech venues

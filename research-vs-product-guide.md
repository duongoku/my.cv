# Research vs. Product: What Makes a Good PhD Topic

## ❌ Product/Engineering Projects (NOT suitable for PhD)

### Characteristics:
- Focus on **building something** (app, system, tool)
- Goal is **commercial viability** or **user adoption**
- Success = **works well**, **people use it**, **scales**
- Novelty is in **features** or **technical implementation**
- Example: "Build an AI memory assistant"

### Why These Are Bad PhD Topics:
1. **No theoretical contribution** - What scientific knowledge does this advance?
2. **Impossible to publish** - Top venues want novel insights, not "we built X"
3. **Thesis committees reject** - "This is engineering, not science"
4. **Job market problems** - Academic positions require research, not engineering

---

## ✅ Research-Grade PhD Topics

### Characteristics:
- Focus on **answering a question** about human behavior, AI systems, or their interaction
- Goal is **advancing scientific understanding**
- Success = **new knowledge**, **theoretical framework**, **generalizable insights**
- Novelty is in **theoretical contribution** or **methodological innovation**
- Building systems is **only a means to answer research questions**

### Example Transformations:

| Product Idea ❌ | Research Topic ✅ |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Build a memory assistant app | How do people develop trust in AI systems that capture their personal information over time? |
| Create an AI tutor for students | When does AI tutoring scaffold learning vs. create dependence? A longitudinal study of metacognitive development. |
| Make an attention-tracking wearable | What are the privacy boundaries people draw around biosignal monitoring in different social contexts? |
| Develop a sleep enhancement device | How does sensory stimulation during sleep affect memory consolidation and next-day cognitive performance in natural settings? |

---

## 🎯 Framework for Evaluating PhD Topics

### The 3 Questions Every PhD Topic Must Answer:

**1. What is the KNOWLEDGE GAP?**
- What do we NOT know that your research will reveal?
- Example gap: "We don't know how long-term AI assistance affects human cognitive abilities"

**2. What is the THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION?**
- What theory, framework, or understanding will your research advance?
- Example: "This research will contribute a theory of cognitive offloading in human-AI collaboration"

**3. What is the METHODOLOGICAL INNOVATION?**
- What methods are you developing or adapting?
- Example: "Novel mixed-methods for studying longitudinal human-AI interaction without multi-year studies"

---

## 🔍 Red Flags: When a Topic is NOT Research

### Warning Signs:
- 🔴 **Primary output is a system/app** (not insights about that system)
- 🔴 **Novelty is features/tech** (not scientific understanding)
- 🔴 **Can't identify a specific research question**
- 🔴 **Success measured by adoption/performance** (not knowledge)
- 🔴 **Would fit a startup pitch** more than an academic abstract

### Green Flags: When a Topic IS Research
- 🟢 **Primary output is knowledge** (system is just for data collection)
- 🟢 **Novelty is theoretical/methodological**
- 🟢 **Clear, specific research questions**
- 🟢 **Success measured by publications/citations**
- 🟢 **Would fit a conference abstract** (CHI, CSCW, etc.)

**"Understanding and Supporting the Long-Term Use of Personal Informatics Systems"**
- Research question: How and why do people sustain or abandon use of self-tracking tools over time?
- Contribution: Longitudinal framework for personal informatics adoption
- NOT: "Build a better fitness tracker"

**"The Effects of Algorithmic Management on Worker Autonomy and Wellbeing"**
- Research question: How does AI management affect workers' sense of autonomy?
- Contribution: Theoretical model of algorithmic control and human agency
- NOT: "Build an AI manager app"

**"Parents' Privacy Concerns About Smart Toys for Children"**
- Research question: How do parents conceptualize privacy for connected children's devices?
- Contribution: Privacy framework for IoT in family contexts
- NOT: "Build a safer smart toy"

## 💡 How to Transform Product Ideas Into Research

### The "Research Lens" Technique:

Take a product idea and ask:

1. **Why would people use this?**
   - Research angle: What psychological, social, or cognitive factors drive adoption?

2. **What effects does this have on users?**
   - Research angle: How does this system change human behavior, cognition, or social dynamics?

3. **What are the trade-offs and tensions?**
   - Research angle: What conflicts arise (privacy vs. personalization, automation vs. autonomy)?

4. **What does this reveal about humans/AI?**
   - Research angle: What does interaction with this system tell us about human cognition, social behavior, or AI ethics?

### Example Transformation:

**Product Idea:** AI memory assistant that records everything

**Research Transformations:**

| Angle | Research Question |
|-------|------------------|
| Psychological effects | How does continuous recording affect people's natural memory formation and recall? |
| Privacy & trust | What are the privacy boundaries people draw around AI memory capture, and how do these evolve? |
| Human-AI collaboration | When do people trust AI memory vs. their own memory, and what factors influence this? |
| Long-term effects | Does reliance on AI memory assistants lead to cognitive offloading or cognitive atrophy? |
| Social dynamics | How does shared AI memory affect group collaboration and collective memory? |

## 📝 Checklist: Is This a Research-Grade Topic?

Before committing, ask:

### The Knowledge Test:
- [ ] Can I state in ONE sentence what scientific knowledge this will advance?
- [ ] Can I identify 3+ papers that have studied related questions?
- [ ] Can I explain what's NEW about what I'm proposing?

### The Contribution Test:
- [ ] Is my main output a PAPER (insights, framework, theory)?
- [ ] Would top venues (CHI, IMWUT, CSCW) be interested?
- [ ] Is the system I'm building ONLY a means to collect data/answer questions?

### The Feasibility Test:
- [ ] Can this be done in 3 years?
- [ ] Do I have access to participants/methods needed?
- [ ] Are there ethical approval concerns I can address?

### The Passion Test:
- [ ] Am I excited about answering THIS question (not just building the tech)?
- [ ] Would I be proud of this research 10 years from now?
- [ ] Does this align with my long-term career goals?

## Final Thought

**Product:** "I built X and it works"
**Research:** "I learned Y about humans/AI by building X and studying it"

A great PhD often builds systems, but the system is **only valuable** insofar as it helps answer interesting research questions.

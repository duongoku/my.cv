## Research Vision

I want to build AI systems that genuinely improve people's daily lives. Specifically, I am passionate about developing intelligent interfaces that reduce cognitive load and enhance human memory—helping people remember what matters, retrieve information when they need it, and live with less mental burden.

My proposed PhD research focuses on creating an **AI-powered multimodal memory assistant** for healthy adults: a system that passively captures information from daily life and enables natural, conversational retrieval while preserving user privacy and control.

---

## Background and Motivation

### Personal Experience

During my research on Alzheimer's disease detection using machine learning, I witnessed how devastating memory impairment can be. However, I also realized that even healthy adults struggle with information overload—we constantly forget conversations, documents, ideas, and tasks. While clinical cognitive enhancement is critical, I believe there is enormous untapped potential in helping the general population manage their daily memories more effectively.

### The Problem

In modern life, we continuously encounter information we want to remember, but existing solutions are inadequate:

- **Manual tools** (notes, calendars) require effort we often don't have
- **Search engines** cannot access our personal experiences
- **General AI assistants** (ChatGPT, Claude) lack personal context
- **Professional tools** (Microsoft Recall, Rewind.ai) are platform-specific and raise serious privacy concerns

**The Gap:** We lack intelligent, privacy-aware systems that can passively capture multimodal information from daily life and provide natural, contextual retrieval—while giving users transparency and control.

### Why This Research Matters

This work addresses a universal problem: everyone forgets things they wish they could remember. A personal memory assistant could:

- Reduce **memory anxiety** ("What did my supervisor say about the deadline?")
- Improve **productivity** (quickly find previous work, meeting notes)
- Enhance **wellbeing** (less mental load, more present-moment focus)
- Enable **better knowledge management** (build personal knowledge base over time)

## Proposed Research

### Research Vision

I propose to develop **MemLife**: an AI-powered multimodal memory assistant that helps healthy adults capture, organize, and retrieve information from their daily lives through natural conversation.

**Core Capabilities:**
- **Passive Capture:** Automatically record conversations, documents, screen activity, and context (time, location)
- **Natural Retrieval:** LLM-powered conversational interface ("What book did Mai recommend last week?")
- **Explainable AI:** Show why results are returned, building on my experience with interpretable ML
- **Privacy-First Design:** User control over what's stored, local processing options, transparent data use

### Research Questions

**RQ1 (Capture):** What information do people want to remember from daily life, and how can we capture it passively while respecting privacy?

**RQ2 (Representation):** How can we represent multimodal experiences to enable accurate retrieval and explainable AI?

**RQ3 (Retrieval):** How can users naturally query personal memories using conversational AI?

**RQ4 (Privacy):** How can we design systems that build user trust through transparency and control?

### Methodology

I will employ an **iterative, user-centered design approach** with four major studies:

| Study | Year | Focus | Method | Participants |
|-------|------|-------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | 1 | Memory needs | Diary study + interviews | 30 adults |
| 2 | 1 | Prototype evaluation | 4-week deployment | 20 users |
| 3 | 2 | Longitudinal use | 8-week field study | 50 users |
| 4 | 3 | Comparative validation | Randomized controlled trial | 80 users |

**Key Methods:**
- Mixed-methods research (quantitative usage analysis + qualitative interviews)
- Longitudinal deployment studies
- A/B testing of features
- Privacy and trust surveys

### Expected Contributions

**Scientific Contributions:**
- Framework for multimodal personal memory capture and retrieval
- Privacy and trust principles for AI memory systems
- Explainable AI methods for personal information retrieval
- Longitudinal understanding of memory assistant adoption

**Practical Contributions:**
- Open-source memory assistant system
- Design guidelines for memory technologies
- Privacy toolkit for personal AI applications

**Societal Impact:**
- Reduce cognitive load and memory anxiety for millions
- Democratize advanced AI memory tools
- Advance responsible AI for personal use
